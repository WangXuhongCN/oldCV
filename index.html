About me

I was born in May 1995, and I am currently a 3rd-year PhD candidate at School of Electronic Information and Electrical Engineering, Shanghai Jiao Tong University, advised by Prof. Yupu Yang. I acquired my bachelor degree from School of Electronics and Information Engineering, Sichuan University in 2017.

My research lies at applying variational inference, (graph) neural networks and anomaly detection techniques to cyber-physical systems and industrial complex networks.

Research Interests

Anomaly Detection, Variational Inference, Graph Neural Networks, Graph Embedding, Self-supervised Learning, Encoder-Decoder Networks, Generative Adversarial Networks, Data Dining

Work Experiences

  • Jul. 2019 -- Present, Deep learning software Intern in Intel Asia-Pacific R&D Co., Shanghai, China.

    Working on optimizing deep learning framework with PyTorch and Intel Analytics Zoo. I currently have two projects: a. I used my adVAE model in unsupervised biopsy image recognition, which even exceeds the performance of supervised Resnet-50. b. I am implementing GCN for discovering the rules of chemical reactivity. Since the number of classes is huge, label smoothing and AM-Softmax techniques is used beyond the plain GCN.

  • May. 2018 -- Jan. 2019, Joint Project Engineer for China Southern Power Grid Co., Guangdong, China.

    Working on generalizing anomaly detection technology to the smart power grid. I built a real time online fault detection system to monitor the status of the transformer substation, using RPCA algorithm, SQL database and Flask web development framework.
